Reading Reports on "How to Read a Book "

"How to Read a Book" is a detailed methodology book featuring on a scientific approach to pre-reading, reading and after-reading and everything. It tells us what's reading, four levels of reading and the specific approach to different genres of book. And the whole report unfolds itself with the sequence mentioned above.

What's reading?

As a active process, reading could be compared as a baseball match. Reader is the catcher，writer is the pitcher. A writer writes down something as he throws the ball to the batter either in a implicit or explicit way. It's all up to the writer. But once the writer finishes throwing the ball, his job's done. The reader should prepare himself in a active manner to catch the ball which means to try hard to understand the information and themes behind it.

The purpose of reading could be classified as read for information and read for understanding. The former one is easy to understand. In our daily life, we read the newspaper to get the know different things happened, or a magazine for fun or boosting our energy. These kind of material is something we could fully understand. According to the Input Hypothesis, it's apperceived, comprehended input. While read for understanding involves the material we may not fully understand. It's something beyond our thinking. We may read that for strengthening our understanding and perception.

Four Levels of Reading

The levels of reading includes basic reading, inspection reading, analyzing reading and the above all theme reading.

Basic Reading

The Basic reading is a preparation period that we strive to learn the new words and strength our vocabulary to swipe the obstacle on our way to apperceived input which's something that could attract the reader or if the book's difficult enough to deter the reader's interest or never choose to read. In this level we pursue for the literate ability.

Inspection Reading

The second level which's inspection reading focusing on the systemic skimming reading that's to get a general impression of the book. Systemic skimming traces along the way from the book title, author, preface, category and index. And we may randomly select a chapter that's seemingly related to the book theme and glance over it. We could also glance over the whole book unremittingly, even if you bump up something you don't understand, just for a coarse reading. Here're something we need to be aware of. We should control the reading speed for different types of books and never linger or reverse. But it would raise a problem how to guarantee our understanding.

Analyzing Reading

To answer this question, we come to the third level of reading that is analyzing reading. The analyzing reading also has three stages. The first stage focus on the framework of book. We may first sort out books by types or themes and choose to read by that. Then try to generalize the book with one sentences. Like this book, we may generalize that with one sentence of "It's a detailed methodology book featuring on a scientific approach to pre-reading, reading and after-reading and everything." Now we come to the framework dissecting of the whole book. After one sentence generalization, one paragraph generalization of the elemental contents is needed. But when it comes to complex one we may introduce to outline to dissect to book in a step by step process. Finally we need to find out the problem the author would solve.

The second stage is with explanatory purpose. First we need to find a common view with the author that's like the ball he has tried to throw to us. We need to find the key words, jargons and special words, and try to deducing the meaning from the sentences around. Then we need to find out and analyze the key sentences to get the git of the book. Be aware of the exposition. We should find out the problem that the author's trying to solve and the problem he hasn't solved. The four and the last stage's to reserve to judge to anything. Try to understand the whole book with the step-by-step process and find the theoretical basis of your arguments if you have any. Use the argument to testify the insufficiency of knowledge or faults points.

Theme Reading

Actually after finishing the three levels of reading, we could already have a fully understanding of the book and master the book. The information obtained from a book may not be able to meet our needs. At this time, we need to carry out theme reading.

First, we need to determine which domain we want to know in depth, and list a list based on the scope of the field. For example, if you want to have a deeper understanding of reading skills, then you can put together the Japanese author Okuno Sato's "How to Read a Book Effectively" and "How to Read a Book" to make a theme book. The book on the list can be collected online, or it can be recommended by a friend. In short, it can be placed in the book list as long as it is related to the theme.

Second, we should look at the books collected in the first stage to find out which chapters are most relevant to their theme. After the chapters are found, we create a set of neutral vocabulary based on the content of these chapters, and use these words to summarize the contents of the books in the list, such as "reading", "notes" and so on. Then, use these words to find a theme that summarizes all the books. Around this theme, ask some questions, such as: "What are the reading skills?" "What should I do with my notes?" Based on these questions, find the answers of each author, list them all in one, and record them in a notebook. We can clearly find out the suggestions and methods given by each author under this theme.

Finally, discuss these methods, which solutions are practical? What are too idealistic? The result after the discussion is the biggest gain in our reading of this topic.

Summary

"How to read a book" is a practical book literally telling us a way to read a book. It's with genuine passion and humble attitude offer us a systematic approach to reading and everything. After finishing that, we could master the book and prepare for the next stage of reading output.
